Making Custom Music and Sound Effects

Finding P5's Sound Files

The ps3.cpk/sound folder contains most of the game's music, sound effects, and voices. They're categorized by purpose (battle, dungeon, event, skills) and further broken down into pairs of ACB and AWB files.

What are ACB and AWB?


These archive formats are provided by Criware for the purpose of storing sound files. Much like Persona 3 and 4, the actual sound files inside are in ADX format.

Extracting the AWB
Download and unzip AcbEditor.exe. To unpack an awb file, simply drag the acb file that shares the same name onto the EXE. It will generate a folder containing the ADX files, which you can replace with your own. For example, the bgm.awb contains all the background music. So to change a BGM track, I'll drag bgm.acb onto b>AcbEditor.exe and then browse the newly generated bgm folder for the ADX files.

ADX Files
To listen to ADX files, download and install foobar2000 and vgmstream. This will add a file association for ADX to your computer, so you can click on them to listen to them in foobar2000.
You can create your own looping ADX files by downloading AtomEncd. This program takes a WAV and turns it into an ADX. You'll need to provide exact sample numbers for the beginning and end of your loops. Watch this video for more information on how to do that.
When you've made your new ADX file, replace it in the unpacked folder but keep the original filename of whatever track you're replacing.

Repacking the AWB

At last, all that's left to do is repack your edited ADX files back into an AWB file.
Fortunately, that's probably the easiest step. Simply drag the unpacked folder (in my case bgm) back onto AcbEditor.exe and it will overwrite bgm.acb and bgm.awb with the changes. Then I can simply move it to the sound directory of my mod.cpk to experience the changes ingame!
