Extracting P3P's CPK Files

What's a CPK?

A CPK file is an archive made by Criware that contains the game's content. They can be obtained and extracted several ways, as well as repacked.

In order to modify or swap the game's files, you must unpack the files contained in the CPK to a folder on your computer. Then, after your changes have been made, compress the extracted folder back into a CPK file.

Getting the CPK files from the game
Persona 3 Portable has 2 main CPKs, umd0.cpk and umd1.cpk. You can find them at the PSP_GAME\USRDIR folder of the game's UMD/ISO.

There also exists a MEMST.CPK that is meant to speed up loading times on physical versions of the game by being copied to your system's hard drive. In the case of digital versions, it's useless and you may delete it without any impact whatsoever.

If you have downloaded the game's ISO or UMD file to your computer, one way of getting the CPK files is to extract them using a program called UMDGen. Run the EXE and locate your ISO file. Navigate to the PSP_GAME\USRDIR folder and right click and extract both umd0.cpk and umd1.cpk.

Extracting the CPK contents
Next, you must follow these steps to get the files from umd0.cpk.

Download and extract CriPakGUI.zip. Run CriPakGUI.exe and click the Extract me! button to browse for your CPK file. It will prompt you to choose a location for the extracted contents. Then sit back and wait for it to finish!
